    High profile figures that have dined here

    "Lovely."

    Guuji Yae

    Hmm? I remember this place being quite pleasant when I came here with the Traveler. Ah, the luxurious sashimi and grilled fish makes me imagine the fond memories I had of my master before she unexpectedly left. If you'd like to take me to Uyuu Restaraunt, bring me a plate of their finest grilled mackarel for no charge, would you?

    "I like the dango milk."

    The Almighty Raiden Shogun

    I heard of Uyuu Restaraunt through Miko - she told me that there was another place to enjoy desserts besides that one stall in Inazuma City. Whenever I'm not doing important duties as Shogunate, I'd see myself coming here alone specifically for the Dango Milk and Tricolor Dango. It has always been a hit every time I've been at Uyuu.

    "A shop of golden treasures."

    Mr. Calx

    I came here with the Traveler during the Irodori Festival, and the dishes at this restaraunt has left me with pleasant stories to write about. If you consider coming here, the Crab Roe Kourayaki, Tri-Flavored Skewer, and the quintessential Berry Mizu Manjuu will provide your soul with the optimal satisfication that Uyuu Restaraunt promises to serve. The Mondstadt Special Mushroom Pizza is different from the pizza I normally encounter back in Mondstadt, but I can clearly taste the distinct flairs that the Inazumans incorporated into their special pizza recipe that makes the dish unlike anything you can find in Mondstadt.

    "A good place to sit down and write!"

    Zhenyu

    I came here with Mr. Calx during a book signing for the Yae Publishing House, and while my arms felt tired from signing so much books, the Egg Rolls and Radish and Fish Stew provided much needed comfort for my hungry body and mind.

    "Cozy spot for simple comforts, and business meetings."

    Ningguang

    While there are many extravagant dishes that Uyuu Restaraunt offers, such as the dedacant Butter Crab and cutely presented Invigorating Kitty Meal, I have frankly appreciated the simpler dishes like the Onigiri and Soba Noodles better. I find that the simplicity of these dishes highlight a brighter part of Inazuman cuisine. That being said, I did appreciate the Nigiri quite fondly. Who knew that such a simple concept could be executed so perfectly, with service similar to those given in the Yanshang Teahouse? I may have to host some of my business meetings at Uyuu from now on.

    "Certainly worth the extra mile."

    Zhongli

    Ever since the Shogun opened up Inazuma, I came back to remember the memories of walking down the streets of Inazuma City, remembering the transfer of commodities between the merchants of local Inazumans. That being said, the Dango Milk and Warmth brings back the memories I had with the friends I had in Inazuma, before the Vision Hunt Decree began. Oddly enough, I could say that these 2 dishes tasted the same as I remember, unlike a certain drink I had a long while ago. Perhaps those who shared the memories I had when I had my first sip of Dango Milk and first tounge burn from the miso soup are still there...?
